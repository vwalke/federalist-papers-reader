The Federalist Papers were written to be read one at a time, in the newspaper, by
people who had never seen the Constitution before. You can read them the same way.

If the full set feels daunting, start with these five. They carry the ideas the
rest of the series builds on, and each one stands on its own.

A few tips for reading here: the essays keep their original wording, so give
yourself room to go slowly. Use the clean Reader view if the newspaper column
tires your eyes, and let the short companion notes on each page carry you through
the harder passages. There is no account and no rush — your progress is remembered
in this browser, one paper at a time.
